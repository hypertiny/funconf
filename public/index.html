funconf

Ireland, 24th April 2010

• • •

Funconf is a conference on a bus, in Ireland.

Whilst embarking on a bus-tour of the Irish countryside, 70 attendees will listen to stories from some of the brightest thought leaders of the online world. The emphasis is on getting up close and personal with the speakers. Each talk, or story, will have only places for 35 attendees, creating an intimate, informal atmosphere.

The trip will start with breakfast in Dublin, before attendees embark on one of the three bus ‘tracks’. Along the way, the ‘conference’ will stop in Kilkenny for Lunch and Waterford for the days ‘keynote’, before heading back to Dublin for a dinner and wrap up event in Dublin’s Guinness Gravity bar.

Speakers on the day will include Michael Lopp, senior manager of engineering at Apple, Marshall Kirkpatrick, lead writer on ReadWriteWeb.com, Jonathan Siegel, serial entrepreneur and founder of ELC Technologies and Joe Stump, former Lead Developer of Digg, co-founder of SimpleGeo.

Funconf is presented by Dublin technologists Paul Campbell and Eamon Leonard, in an effort to bring a unique Irish perspective to the world of technology conferences.
